# Dog Training Simulation

## Settings
- **Backyard** - Open grass area for practice
- **Living Room** - Indoor training space with distractions
- **Park** - Public area with other stimuli

## Props/Agents

**Dog (Agent)**
- Attributes: Focus (0-100), Energy (0-100), Trust (0-100), Skill Level (0-100)
- Actions: Sit, Stay, Come, Bark, Ignore, Respond

**Trainer (Agent)**
- Attributes: Patience (0-100), Consistency (0-100), Treat Supply (0-20)
- Actions: Give Command, Reward, Correct, Praise, Ignore Behavior

**Treats (Prop)**
- Attributes: Quantity (decreases with use), Appeal (affects dog motivation)

**Distractions (Prop)**
- Attributes: Intensity (low/medium/high) - reduces dog's Focus

## Core Interactions

When **Trainer** uses "Give Command":
- If Dog's Focus > 60 and Energy > 30, Dog performs action correctly
- Success increases Dog's Skill Level (+5) and Trust (+3)
- Failure decreases Dog's Focus (-10)

When **Trainer** uses "Reward" with Treats:
- Dog's Trust increases (+10)
- Dog's Skill Level for last action increases (+8)
- Treat Supply decreases (-1)
- Dog's Energy decreases slightly (-5)

When **Distractions** are present:
- Dog's Focus decreases proportionally to Intensity
- High Distraction Intensity + Low Skill = Dog uses "Ignore" action
- Trainer's Consistency determines effectiveness of commands despite distractions

When **Trainer's Patience** drops below 40:
- Dog's Trust decreases (-5 per command)
- Commands become less effective (50% success rate)

When **Dog's Energy** drops below 20:
- All Skill performances decrease (-20 effectiveness)
- Dog defaults to "Ignore" action

**Trainer's Consistency** above 70:
- Dog's Skill Level increases faster (+2 bonus per success)
- Dog's Focus recovers faster (+5 per session)
